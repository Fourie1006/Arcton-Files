\

\ This resource is designed to be practical. Use it exactly as written.
\ Copy the templates. Replace the variables. Run the searches. Collect evidence.

\ \ SECTION A \'97 SELF-ASSESSMENT (TRAIT-BASED FIRST)

\ \'95 O*NET Interest Profiler: https://www.onetonline.org/
\ \'95 VIA Character Strengths: https://www.viacharacter.org/

\ \ Note on \'93type\'94 tests: They can be fun and motivating, but they tend to lock you into categories.\ If you want something more flexible, focus on OCEAN-style traits (you move along a spectrum, not into a box).

\ \ SECTION B \'97 CAREER REALITY CHECK (WHAT THE JOB ACTUALLY IS)

\ \'95 CareerOneStop (global-friendly): https://www.careeronestop.org/
\ \'95 U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Handbook: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/
\ \'95 Prospects (UK): https://www.prospects.ac.uk/

\ \ Copy/Paste Search Prompts:
\ \'93day in the life of a [job title]\'94
\ \'93what I wish I knew before becoming a [job title]\'94
\ \'93entry level [job title] responsibilities\'94
\ \'93[job title] stress levels reality\'94
\ \'93[job title] skills you need to succeed\'94

\ \ SECTION C \'97 INFORMATIONAL CHAT TEMPLATE (WARM + CONVERSATIONAL)

\ \ Hey [Name],

\ \ I hope you\'92re doing well. I\'92m exploring different paths after school and I came across your work as a [Job Title].\ I\'92m genuinely curious about what the role is like in real life, not just what the internet says.

\ \ If you\'92d be open to it, could I ask you a few questions in a quick 10\'9615 minute chat?\ I\'92m especially interested in how you built skills like [Skill 1] and [Skill 2], and what you think matters most when someone is starting out.

\ \ No pressure at all. Even a few lines by message would help a lot.\ Thank you for considering it.

\ \ \'97 [Your Name]
\ [Optional: School/City]

\ \ Great questions to ask:
\ 1) What surprised you most about the job once you started?
\ 2) What does a \'93good week\'94 vs \'93bad week\'94 look like?
\ 3) What skills matter most day-to-day?
\ 4) If you could restart at 17\'9619, what would you do differently?
\ 5) What kind of person thrives in this work?

\ \ SECTION D \'97 LOW-RISK EXPERIENCE (BUILD EVIDENCE FAST)

\ \'95 Volunteer once a week in an environment related to the field.
\ \'95 Take one free intro course and finish it (proof > browsing).
\ \'95 Shadow for half a day if possible, even informally.

\ \ Free learning platforms:
\ \'95 Coursera: https://www.coursera.org/
\ \'95 edX: https://www.edx.org/
\ \'95 OpenLearn: https://www.open.edu/openlearn/

\ \ SECTION E \'97 DECISION FRAMEWORK (ONE PAGE)

\ 1) Write your top 3 interests and top 3 strengths.
\ 2) Pick 5 careers and research daily reality (not salary first).
\ 3) Speak to 2 people in 2 fields. Capture notes.
\ 4) Do 1 low-risk experience in 1 field.
\ 5) Review: what energised you, what drained you, what you\'92d repeat.

\ \ If you want to go faster, paste this into any AI tool and fill in the brackets:

\ \ \'93I\'92m a [age] student choosing a major. My interests are [x]. My strengths are [y]. My values are [z].\ Suggest 6 career paths to explore and for each: daily tasks, key skills, starter steps I can take this month,\ and one low-risk way to test fit.\'94
