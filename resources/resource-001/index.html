RESOURCE 001 — PATHFINDING TOOLS

COMPANION: FILE 001 • STATUS: PUBLIC RELEASE

This resource is designed to be practical. Use it exactly as written. Copy the templates. Replace the variables. Run the searches. Collect evidence.

SECTION A — SELF-ASSESSMENT (TRAIT-BASED FIRST) • O*NET Interest Profiler: https://www.onetonline.org/ • VIA Character Strengths: https://www.viacharacter.org/ Note on “type” tests: They can be fun, but they often lock you into categories. If you want something more flexible, focus on OCEAN-style traits (you move along a spectrum, not into a box). SECTION B — CAREER REALITY CHECK (WHAT THE JOB ACTUALLY IS) • CareerOneStop (global-friendly): https://www.careeronestop.org/ • U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Handbook: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/ • Prospects (UK): https://www.prospects.ac.uk/ SECTION C — COPY/PASTE SEARCH PROMPTS (USE THESE) 1) “Day in the life” prompt: day in the life of a [job title] tasks responsibilities stressors favorite parts 2) “What I wish I knew” prompt: what I wish I knew before becoming a [job title] + mistakes + reality 3) “Study path” prompt: what subjects should I take to become a [job title] in [country] SECTION D — WARM OUTREACH TEMPLATE (INFORMATIONAL CHAT / SHADOWING) Subject: Quick question about your work as a [JOB TITLE] Hi [NAME], Hope you’re doing well. I’m currently exploring possible study/career paths and your work as a [JOB TITLE] stood out. I’m trying to understand what the role actually looks like in real life, especially the parts people don’t see from the outside (day-to-day tasks, skills that matter most, what you enjoy, what’s tough). If you’re open to it, could I ask you 5–10 quick questions over a short call — or even just by message/email? No pressure at all. Even a few bullet points would help massively. Either way, thanks for your time — and respect for the work you do. Best, [YOUR NAME] [OPTIONAL: SCHOOL / CITY]
