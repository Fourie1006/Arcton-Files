FILE 001 — THE FIRST QUESTION

TYPE: Dialogue / Reflection • STATUS: Sanitised • CLEARANCE: Public Release

The first question is never asked loudly.

It arrives quietly — usually late, usually alone — and it sounds deceptively simple:

“What do you want to do after school?”

For most students, it feels premature. Abstract. Unfair. A decision framed as permanent when nothing else in life feels settled yet.

Internal records recovered from the Arcton Institute show that Elena encountered this question early — not as pressure from others, but as a tension she couldn’t ignore. Rather than rushing toward an answer, she documented her process.

What follows is not a prescription.
It is a method.

A way of approaching uncertainty without pretending it doesn’t exist.

SECTION A — SELF-ASSESSMENT (TRAIT-BASED FIRST)

Elena started where most advisors quietly agree you should begin: with the self, not with job titles. Instead of asking what job she should choose, she asked how she actually thinks, reacts, focuses, and cares.

  • O*NET Interest Profiler — onetonline.org
  • VIA Character Strengths — viacharacter.org

Note on “type” tests: they can be motivating, but they tend to lock you into categories. Arcton research favors trait-based models, especially OCEAN (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism). Traits exist on spectrums — you move along them over time.

SECTION B — CAREER REALITY CHECK (WHAT THE JOB ACTUALLY IS)

Once Elena had language for herself, she turned outward — not toward prestige, toward evidence. She cross-referenced roles using three databases:

  • CareerOneStop (global-friendly) — careeronestop.org
    Practical summaries of responsibilities, skills, and pathways.
  • Occupational Outlook Handbook (BLS) — bls.gov/ooh
    Deep breakdowns of job growth, stressors, and education requirements.
  • Prospects (UK) — prospects.ac.uk
    Useful for early-career realities and role expectations.

SECTION C — COPY/PASTE PROMPTS (USE THESE EXACTLY)

These prompts reduce generic answers and extract practical reality. Replace [JOB TITLE] and [COUNTRY] only.

1) Role Reality Prompt You are an experienced [JOB TITLE] with at least 5 years in the field. Describe: - a realistic day-to-day workflow - key skills that actually matter - common stressors people underestimate - parts of the job that are unexpectedly rewarding Avoid generic descriptions. 2) Pre-Decision Reflection Prompt From the perspective of a [JOB TITLE]: What do most students misunderstand before entering this field? What would you do differently if you were choosing again today? Be honest, not motivational. 3) Study Pathway Prompt Outline a realistic study and early-career pathway to become a [JOB TITLE] in [COUNTRY]. Include: - recommended subjects - optional vs essential qualifications - early experience that improves long-term outcomes

SECTION D — WARM OUTREACH TEMPLATE (INFORMATIONAL CHAT / SHADOWING)

Elena did not cold-message. She contextualized. Use this exactly. Replace only the variables.

Subject: Quick question about your work as a [JOB TITLE] Hi [NAME], I hope you’re well. I’m currently exploring possible study and career directions, and your work as a [JOB TITLE] stood out to me. I’m trying to understand what the role looks like beyond job descriptions — the skills that actually matter, the parts you enjoy, and the challenges people don’t usually talk about. If you’d be open to it, I’d love to ask you a few short questions — over a quick call or even by message/email. No pressure at all. Even a few insights would be incredibly helpful. Either way, thank you for your time — and respect for the work you do. Best, [YOUR NAME] [OPTIONAL: SCHOOL / CITY]
